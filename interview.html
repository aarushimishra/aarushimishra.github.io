Traveling Habits

  • What is your preferred way of commuting? Do you use your own transport? Prefer Uber/ Lyft? Public transport? Reason for using the preferred method.
  • Being a professional, do you commute on a daily basis? Do you plan it beforehand?
  • What does your weekend look like? Do you stay indoors or step out? If later is applicable, how do you commute?
  • Do you use any navigation apps? Like Google Maps, Apple Maps, or Waze, if so when do you use it the most?
  • Have you ever used any parking finder app? If yes, which one? If no, why not?

Finding Parking Spots

  • Do the places you frequently visit offer parking? If not, where do you park?
  • Does your office offer parking? Or do you reserve any spots in advance? (Monthly passes)
  • Do you have to commute further after parking your vehicle? How much do you walk and how much time does it take?
  • How do you estimate your ETA to your destination?
  • How much time do you spend finding a parking space? Do you account for this in your ETA?
  • How do you keep yourself informed about the time left for your parked vehicle?

Insights

  • Users with families own multiple vehicles. They choose to travel individually for work but prefer using one vehicle when traveling otherwise.
  • People living in metropolitan areas often opt for public transportation over their personal vehicles to reach their destinations. This saves them money and time for parking.
  • Constant search for free or affordable parking options as close to their destination as possible. They use Google maps to find parking spots and estimated walking distance.
  • Notification bomb! One of the users who uses a parking app mentioned that it became a nuisance as there was no way to limit or control the amount of notifications while he was driving.
  • The uncertainty of where to park adds to the driver's anxiety. Especially when they are travelling to unfamiliar and unsafe areas.
  • Users were at times compelled to park further away from their destination because they were unaware of nearby parking spaces.
  • The local street rules and signs are extremely confusing. No standardisation existed.
  • Non standardised parking meters make it stressful for older users to use.
