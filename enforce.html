Parking Enforce

Mobile application for parking enforcement officers.

To comply with my non-disclosure agreement, I have changed and obfuscated confidential information in this case study. The information in this case study is my own and does not necessarily reflect the views of Fybr.

Parking violations are a common problem in most of the major cities. Fair enforcement of parking rules can help keep streets clear and safe – addressing hazards such as double parked vehicles, blocked handicapped zones or fire hydrants – and can decrease congestion in high-demand parking areas.

The enforce app helps the policy enforcement officers (or PEOs) in better managing the violations by alerting them as they occur. It allows the PEOs to take suitable action for the violation from their mobile devices saving them fuel costs and time.

Senior User Experience Designer | Fybr

Being an individual, I hypothesised and designed the end-to-end experience of the enforce app. I worked closely with the subject matter expert and developers.

April 2018 to October 2018

What is digital parking enforcement?

Traditionally, enforcement officers patrol assigned beats to look for parking violations and take appropriate actions to those violations.

Digital parking enforcement is technology that enables cities to monitor parked cars, identify if cars are complying with parking rates and rules, and issue parking citations. With online enforcement, PEOs can access an enforcement system in real-time using a connected handheld device.With a complete view of all violations as they are occurring, officers can prioritise their efforts based on violation density, severity of violation, or safety hazards such as double parking or parking in front of hydrants.

Research

The design process for this project was challenging to say the least. I did not have any existing apps I could visit for market research. My user base was niche and existed in another continent which I couldn’t travel to. I was given a list of data points that needed to be captured. Without user research, I had to make design decisions based upon intuition and assumptions alone. This was not ideal as waiting until release to get feedback could waste resources, lose trust, and lead to irreversible mistakes. Hence, the only process left was to work with my US team and iterate and validate with them.
