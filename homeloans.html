Home Loans

Moving the homeloan application process online.

Owning a house bears an important aspect in the life of every Indian. But, given the current property rates it is not possible for everyone to purchase a house as most of the Indian population belongs to the middle class. Here’s when the financial institutions come to an aid for the common man by providing them with home-loans. But, acquiring a home-loan is not an easy task; it is a complex and intimidating process.

At housing.com's home-loans portal we tried to simplify this process of home-loan application. We tried to create a portal where the user can view, compare and apply for a home-loan.

UI/UX Designer | Housing.com

Being the sole ux designer on this project, I researched, hypothesised, designed and tested the end-to-end experience of the home-loan application process. I worked closely with the project manager and developers to produce a MVP which can be later linked to the ‘buying section’ of the website.

January 2015 to June 2015

What is a homeloan?

A home-loan, also known as mortgage, is an amount of money borrowed by an individual from financial institutions (either banks or companies). The borrower has to pay the bank the loan amount with interest to the institution in the form of EMI's (Easy Monthly Instalments) over a period of time.

The Challenge

Technological innovations of recent years are now shaping how we interact with the world. More and more services are moving from offline to online. The financial industry is one such industry embracing this process.
Gone are the conventional days of standing in bank queues to apply for loans and go through the cumbersome process which includes multiple trips to banks, including the depth of documentation.

The challenge was to design a marketplace that allows easy search, comparison and application of home-loans for the web, mobile-web and android platforms.

Proto Persona

Since we were on a tight deadline I created a proto persona to represent the majority of target users. This was done to guide the design decisions for the development and creation of the solution.

User Journey Map

The user journey displays a typical process followed to obtain a home-loan by Siddhartha. I created the map to put my field research into a flow and extract the opportunities from Siddhartha’s journey.

User Stories

To get started with my initial designs, I needed to come up with as many possible situations of what Siddhartha would potentially need from a solution. To organise these ideas I created user stories and cluster them into epics. This exercise was helpful for me to come up with product features.
